Week 2 Discussion

1) What I can do in my webpages is keeping the code clean and using the right elements where they belong. Elements like alt tags, headings, lists, forms (label inputs with meaning!), ect. Elements the reader goes through should be able to cue the user on what to do next, without nearby visual aids.

2) One thing that I saw in a video was how dynamic content was relayed to the user. If new content is injected, there is a way for that content to be read to, or alert, the user. But one thing to keep in mind is that if content is updated in a rapid way, it could result in lost information to the user, or even a garbled mess. To work with this, content should be updated in a way that the user will be able to absorb new information at their own pace, resulting in all information being available to each type of user.

3) Refreshing Braille displays are one type of device made for accessability. The work they do is giving a physical way for people to read (through Braille) what is currently on a computer screen. I saw a video where a man used it to read what his iPhone was doing, and also to run commands to his iPhone in turn.

Apart from the Refreshing Braille displays, most phones have begun to adopt some form of Accessibility. Blackberry phones have options for hearing, vision, mobility, cognitive, and speech disabilities. iPhone lists hearing, vision, and cognitive solutions as well. Android is working toward the same direction as the others, with a special note to Google's work in general. They are coming out with ChromeVox which will be a screenreader built for web, integrated right into Chrome. Additionally they have improved most of their services, and even put in the automatic subtitle generation for YouTube.

4) One app I came across during my research was WalkyTalky. It is an app that updates the user on their location, and can even set a destination and work in a Walking directions mode. Seems pretty cool. Another app is Wireless Accessibility, apparently 10 features in one app: Phone, Contacts, SMS, Alarm, Calendar, Email, Web, Where am I, Apps and Settings. It simplifies the interface and uses voice to help the user traverse the phone easier.
